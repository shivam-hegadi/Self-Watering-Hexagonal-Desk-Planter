Desk Planter for Thingondesk Hackclub Program

# Self-Watering Hexagonal Desk Planter Development Journal

## Journal 1: Initial Design Concept & Base Sketch

I created a sketch for a hexagonal planter with a water reservoir and smaller plant pods inside. The hexagon shape fits better on a desk and allows for more plants than traditional rectangular designs. The concept relies on water moving up from the reservoir into the soil through capillary action.

<img width="719" height="628" alt="image6" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/3a3524d5-cbf3-41dd-9efe-a2019a35668b" />

## Journal 2: Extrusion & Hollow Shell Structure

I extruded the hexagon to give it depth and used the shell feature to hollow out the interior for water storage. I added a transparent window on the side so the water level is visible without taking the planter apart. This gives me a functional container that can hold water safely.

<img width="719" height="628" alt="image4" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/54ef654e-4268-4742-893d-89bba97e4f9a" />

## Journal 3: Latch System for Pod Stability

I designed latches at the bottom of the container to keep the plant pods in place and prevent them from shifting around. I positioned the seven pods in a circle pattern so each one has its own space. This makes the design modular so individual pods can be replaced if needed.

<img width="719" height="628" alt="image3" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/17c390dd-4400-4b2a-8a65-c951cef13890" />

## Journal 4: Lid Assembly & Pod Fabrication

I created a lid for the top to give the planter a finished appearance. I made each plant pod using the same extrusion and shell method so they all have consistent wall thickness. The lid and pods fit together cleanly and look like a complete product.

<img width="719" height="628" alt="image10" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/3e34181b-c1dc-42ed-84a2-6542b07d0ca4" />

## Journal 5: Irrigation Holes & Structural Lip Design

I added small holes to the bottom of each pod to allow water to seep up into the soil. I also added a lip around the top edge of each pod to keep the soil above the water level and prevent waterlogging. The holes are spaced around the perimeter of each pod for even water distribution.

<img width="334" height="566" alt="image11" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/162da1c6-0f23-450f-95ba-46ddf4f712fb" />

## Journal 6: Pod Array Assembly & Tolerance Refinement

I assembled all seven pods in the lid and noticed they were touching each other. I used Fusion's interference detection to confirm the collision and adjusted the lip height to eliminate contact while keeping the pods elevated above water. This small change prevents air flow problems and potential damage during assembly.

<img width="595" height="646" alt="image1" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/83f190da-63bd-4a4c-919b-224e26eaae10" />

## Journal 7: Water Level Indicator Mechanism

I added a bright yellow floating marble to the lid that drops as the water level falls. The marble sits in a track and provides a clear visual indicator of when refilling is needed. This mechanical system requires no batteries and gives immediate feedback on water status.

<img width="157" height="406" alt="image9" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/eedf826e-5d30-4db8-b3b9-5ef6f2b6d163" />

## Journal 8: Tolerance Refinement on Bottom Latch Holders

I adjusted the latch geometry to ensure there is no collision with the pods during assembly. I refined the clearances to approximately 0.5 to 1.0 millimeters and tested multiple assembly scenarios. This prevents jamming and ensures reliable assembly even with manufacturing variation.

<img width="1612" height="192" alt="image5" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/eaedf315-3ecc-410b-80db-72e27bfb5e0f" />

## Journal 9: Side-Mounted Water Access Port

I added a pocket on the side of the container for easy water refilling without disassembling the lid and pods. The pocket directs water into the reservoir without spillage. This feature significantly streamlines the maintenance process.

<img width="280" height="365" alt="image2" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ff7fcdf0-39ef-4b6e-b33e-a8da0b9b2990" />

## Journal 10: Interior Silicone Seal Application

I applied silicone sealant to all interior surfaces to prevent water leakage through the 3D printed layers and seams. I paid special attention to layer transitions and structural features that might allow water to escape. Silicone was chosen for its flexibility and resistance to moisture degradation.

<img width="1440" height="960" alt="image8" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/76ae1b92-b9f9-4644-9a7b-219b4200c8b2" />

## Journal 11: System Assembly, Soil Installation & Functional Testing

I assembled all the components, filled the pods with soil and seeds, and poured water into the side pocket to test the system. The water successfully moved up into the soil through the irrigation holes and maintained appropriate moisture levels. The yellow marble descended as expected, confirming the irrigation system works correctly.
